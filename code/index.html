Belt and Road Initiative: Development Paths in Comparison

This project explores whether countries participating in the Belt and Road Initiative show different development trajectories from comparable non-BRI countries. Using World Development Indicators, we examine changes in economic performance, infrastructure, technology adoption, governance, and trade over time.

Project Introduction

The Belt and Road Initiative, launched in 2013, is one of the most visible international development frameworks of the last decade. It is often associated with infrastructure investment, trade connectivity, and regional cooperation, but its broader relationship with national development remains difficult to assess. Our project uses a comparative and data-driven approach to explore whether BRI countries differ from non-BRI countries across multiple indicators rather than a single economic metric.

Instead of asking whether every country follows the same path, the visualization highlights diversity across countries and regions. Users can compare long-term temporal trends, group-level distributions, and relationships between key indicators through an interactive storytelling website.

Main Visualization

Time Trends Across Countries

This section presents the main time-series visualization, showing how key development indicators evolve over time across Belt and Road Initiative (BRI) and non-BRI countries. Covering the period from 2000 onward, it enables a long-term view of economic and social change.

Particular attention is given to the period after 2013, when the BRI was formally introduced. By comparing trends before and after this point, users can explore whether BRI countries exhibit distinct development trajectories relative to non-BRI countries.

The interactive controls allow users to switch between indicators and filter by region or country, making it possible to uncover heterogeneous patterns and identify whether observed trends are consistent across regions or driven by specific cases.

Solid line = BRI average, dashed line = non-BRI average. Vertical line marks the year 2013.

Comparative Analysis

BRI and Non-BRI Countries in Cross-Section

Baseline Comparison

This section compares the distribution of development indicators between Belt and Road Initiative (BRI) countries and non-BRI countries for a selected baseline year. Rather than focusing only on average values, we use distribution-based visualizations such as boxplots to highlight variability within each group.

The chart above shows how GDP per capita differs across regions participating in the BRI. European BRI countries tend to exhibit higher median income levels, while African and Asian BRI regions show lower medians but varying degrees of dispersion. This suggests that development within BRI regions is not uniform and may depend on regional economic structure.

By allowing users to adjust the comparison year, this view makes it possible to explore how disparities evolve over time and whether BRI participation is associated with convergence or divergence in economic outcomes.
